Celestia 2D – Object Creation Guide

This page explains how objects behave in the simulation based on their mass, radius, and density. Use this guide to understand what each object type represents and how it will interact once placed into the universe.

How Objects Are Classified

Dust

Appears when:
• Mass = 1e-6 • OR radius is less than 5

Dust behaves like tiny particles that get easily pulled by gravity. Useful for forming accretion disks or dust clouds.

Planet

Density between 0 and 5.

Regular planets with stable gravitational interactions. They orbit larger bodies and work well for solar system setups.

Giant Planet

Density between 0 and 5 AND radius ≥ 50.

Very massive planets. They pull in smaller objects strongly and can dominate orbital systems.

Star

Density between 5 and 20.

Strong gravitational pull. Stars are ideal for creating stable orbits or multiple-star systems.

Neutron Star

Density between 20 and 50.

Small but extremely dense. They attract objects very rapidly and can create chaotic movement if placed too close to others.

Black Hole

Density 50 or higher.

Very strong gravitational field. Objects spiral inward and merge if they get too close. Best used for accretion disks or galaxy centers.

How Physics Works

  • Gravity pulls objects based on mass and distance.
  • Objects merge when they get close enough.
  • The new object keeps the total mass of both objects.
  • Radius is recalculated based on combined volume.
  • Momentum is preserved, so new objects continue moving smoothly.
  • Some collisions generate a shockwave animation.
  • Objects with the right speed form stable orbits.

Tips for Creating Interesting Systems

  • Use low density (0–5) for planets.
  • Use medium density (5–20) for stars.
  • Use very high density (50+) only for special cases (black holes).
  • Place dust around stars to form accretion disks.
  • Spread objects out to avoid instant merging.
  • Use different masses and radii for more dynamic movement.
